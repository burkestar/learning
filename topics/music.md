# Music

## Music Theory

### Books

**Guitar-Specific Theory:**

- **[Music Theory for Guitarists by Tom Kolb](https://www.amazon.com/Music-Theory-Guitarists-Everything-Wanted/dp/063406651X)** (Hal Leonard) — Probably the most universally recommended guitar theory book. Covers scales, modes, chord construction, and progressions with clear diagrams, exercises, and audio tracks.
- **[The Practical Guide to Modern Music Theory for Guitarists by Joseph Alexander](https://www.amazon.com/Practical-Guide-Modern-Music-Theory/dp/1789330971)** — Takes a guitarist's perspective throughout, highly regarded for being accessible without dumbing things down.
- **[Idiot's Guides: Guitar Theory by David Hodges](https://www.amazon.com/Idiots-Guides-Guitar-Theory-David/dp/1615646086)** — Excellent visual approach with fretboard diagrams, tabs, and chord charts.
- **[Fretboard Logic SE by Bill Edwards](https://www.amazon.com/Fretboard-Logic-SE-Reasoning-Positions/dp/0962477060)** — Great for understanding the CAGED system and how theory maps to the guitar neck.

**General Theory (Instrument-Agnostic but Excellent):**

- **[Idiot's Guides: Music Theory by Michael Miller](https://www.amazon.com/Idiots-Guides-Music-Theory-3E/dp/1465451676)** — Frequently cited as the single best general theory book. He explains the *why* behind concepts.

**For Intermediate/Advanced Growth:**

- **[The Advancing Guitarist by Mick Goodrick](https://www.amazon.com/Advancing-Guitarist-Mick-Goodrick/dp/0881885894)** — Challenges conventional thinking about improvisation and chord voicings.
- **[Chord Chemistry by Ted Greene](https://www.amazon.com/Chord-Chemistry-Ted-Greene/dp/0898986966)** — Deep dive into harmony and chord voicings for expanding your songwriting palette.

### Websites

- **[JustinGuitar.com — Practical Music Theory](https://www.justinguitar.com/classes/practical-music-theory-course-fast-hands-on)** — Justin Sandercoe's theory course is outstanding for guitarists with real-world examples and singer/songwriter sensibility.
- **[Musictheory.net](https://www.musictheory.net/)** — Clean, interactive lessons covering fundamentals from note reading to chord progressions.
- **[MusicTheoryForGuitar.com](https://www.musictheoryforguitar.com/)** — Specifically designed for guitarists with lessons that show how to apply theory to real playing. Includes [free resources and ebooks](https://www.musictheoryforguitar.com/guitar-music-theory-free-resources.html).
- **[Teoria.org](https://www.teoria.com/)** — Rigorous tutorials and ear training exercises.
- **[HubGuitar.com — Music Theory](https://hubguitar.com/music-theory)** — Interactive lessons with scales, fretboard navigation, and theory.
- **[Toby Rush's Theory Pages](https://tobyrush.com/theorypages/)**
